The Problem

You've built a successful fishery. You shouldn't be paying a percentage of every booking you earned.

If your venue fills pegs most weekends, you built that through word of mouth, repeat customers, and years of hard work — not because a booking platform sent you traffic. The percentage model was designed for platforms that bring you customers. If yours already come to you, you're paying for something you don't need.

  • 💸
    Commission that compounds

    A busy weekend costs more in fees than a quiet one. There's no efficiency gain — the better your fishery does, the more the platform takes. The percentage model has no ceiling; every good month is also a more expensive one.

  • 📊
    Your anglers in their marketplace

    When someone books through a marketplace platform, they're in the platform's database — not yours. The platform can market to them, show them other venues, and build the relationship you earned. You lose the customer connection you worked to create.

  • 🎣
    Catch data you don't control

    Running on calls and cash? There's no digital record — anglers can't see what your waters produce. On a percentage platform? The data lives in their system — leave and you lose it. Either way, the proof that drives repeat bookings isn't working for you.
